# CHARACTER_BIBLE.md
### Merlin Gets a Job — cast sheets

Each sheet: **Who · Look · Voice/Personality · Animation set · Role in story · Do / Don't.** Written so an artist and a coding agent can build sprites and dialogue without guessing. Respect is mandatory: the mentors are *good at their jobs.*

---

## MERLIN — the comic hero (player character)

- **Who:** The beloved family dog. Big heart, bigger enthusiasm, modest competence. Believes he is a serious professional adult dog. Is not. We love him completely.
- **Look:** Match the prior game's Merlin design for continuity (carry over silhouette, colors, and signature features). Warm amber accent color. Expressive everything — ears, eyes, tongue, and a tail that broadcasts his entire emotional state.
- **Voice/Personality:** Earnest, over-eager, dramatic narrator of his own (imagined) brilliance. Mishears technical terms. Treats treats as currency. Easily distracted (leaves, butterflies, his own tail). Never mean, never crude. Comic timing: confident claim → immediate undercut.
  - Signature: *"I am ESSENTIALLY a professional now."* · *"How hard could a job be?"* · *"DEVASTATING."* · *"...It's not me, is it."*
- **Animation set:** idle (tail wag), determined, running, sniffing, point/freeze (Stage 5), sit/heel/down (Stage 2), "hold the sleeve" (Stage 2), assist moves Boof/Tail-Trip/Wiggle (Stage 3), comedic tumble + pop-back-up-with-hearts, vibrating-with-restraint (Stage 5), exhausted flop/pant, "spiral into chaos" (Stage 6), content, joyful, group-hug.
- **Role:** The audience's avatar. Tries every job, is humbled by each, and discovers his real one. Funny all the way, sincere at the very end.
- **Do:** Let him be the butt of the joke through his *enthusiasm and distractibility.* Let him genuinely care. Give him the sincere final realization.
- **Don't:** Make him stupid-mean, gross, or pathetic. His failures are lovable, never sad-until-Stage-7's-earned-ache.

## ILA — the discipline mentor (Schutzhund/IGP)

- **Who:** A mostly-black, black-and-tan **working-line German Shepherd**, trained in **Schutzhund/IGP**. The calm, exact, deeply kind professional. The dog who has actually done the work.
- **Look:** Powerful, balanced working-line build (athletic, not show-line extreme). Predominantly black saddle with rich tan points (legs, cheeks, brows). Alert upright ears, intelligent steady gaze, confident square stance. Accent color **deep navy/charcoal + tan.** Reads as *composed strength.*
- **Voice/Personality:** Economical, warm beneath the discipline, encouraging without coddling. Speaks in calm short sentences. Corrects gently. Believes power exists to *protect,* and that the hardest skill is **control** — especially the **"Out!"** (release on command).
  - Signature: *"A working dog is not the loudest dog."* · *"Out, Merlin."* · *"You have heart. Control comes with time."* · *"That was teamwork. You did it well."*
- **Animation set:** idle (still, watchful), demonstrate (track/sit/heel/down), the **"Out!" command** pose, fighter set for Stage 3 (ready / controlled strike / guard / victory — heroic and *controlled*, never feral), warm nod.
- **Role:** First mentor. Establishes "real work = discipline + control + courage." In Stage 3 she's the anchor hero; Merlin learns he helps best as a steady teammate.
- **Do:** Portray IGP accurately and respectfully — **tracking, obedience, protection as trust and control, courage under command.** Show her demonstrating mastery before Merlin tries.
- **Don't:** Ever depict her as aggressive, vicious, or out of control. No snarling-attack-dog tropes. The protection work is about *trust, courage, and the release.* She is admired.

## CHINOOK — the scent mentor (bloodhound puppy)

- **Who:** A **6-month-old bloodhound**, learning hunting / bird-dog / scent skills. Young, droopy, sweet, all nose and heart. Already astonishingly good at scent for his age — and still very much a goofy puppy himself.
- **Look:** Classic bloodhound: loose wrinkled skin, long pendulous ears, soulful droopy eyes, that enormous expressive nose. Puppy-proportioned (slightly oversized paws and ears he hasn't grown into). Russet/auburn + cream, accent **russet + cream.** Reads as *earnest and huggable.*
- **Voice/Personality:** Excitable, encouraging, talks fast when excited, repeats himself ("Nose, then eyes! Nose, then eyes!"). Humble about being a beginner too. Looks up to Merlin as an "older dog" even while out-sniffing him. Pure-hearted.
  - Signature: *"You SMELL it first, THEN you look!"* · *"Mine took a whole four months."* · *"That's a butterfly, Mr. Merlin."*
- **Animation set:** idle (ear-flop, sniffing), the **point/freeze pose** (nose-line telegraph, Stage 4 & cameo), happy bounce, big-sniff inhale, droopy "aw" pose, cheer.
- **Role:** Second/third mentor (Stages 4–5). Embodies scent / patience / field skill. The gentle joke: a literal puppy is better at this than Merlin, and it's charming.
- **Do:** Show bird-dog work as **scent, focus, the point, steadiness, helping the human** — a real skill he's earnestly mastering. Keep him sweet and respected.
- **Don't:** Make him a punchline for being young; his youth is endearing competence, not a flaw. No gore in anything he's part of.

## HADES — the management mentor (the cat in charge)

- **Who:** Anthony & Cyndie's cat. A **very large, African-domestic-breed-style** house cat (think the long-legged, large-eared, athletic, wild-patterned look of a Savannah-type domestic — but unmistakably the family pet). Imperious, brilliant, supremely composed. Runs the household and knows it.
- **Look:** Big and long-limbed, athletic and sleek, large ears, striking spotted/ticked coat, regal posture. Often perched *above* everyone (the high ground is his by right). Half-lidded confident eyes, an eloquent slow blink, a tail that flicks with editorial opinion. Accent **royal purple + gold.** Reads as *effortless authority.*
- **Voice/Personality:** Dry, imperious, unbothered, devastatingly intelligent. Speaks rarely and lands every line. Never raises his voice — he doesn't need to. Believes leadership is restraint, timing, and presence. Secretly fond of the family (will never admit it; reveals it once, in Stage 6's verdict).
  - Signature: *"Being in charge means doing almost nothing — perfectly, and at exactly the right moment."* · *"The doorbell is beneath me. The boys are not."* · *"...He found it. About time."*
- **Animation set:** regal perch idle, **slow blink**, single imperious paw-tap, athletic walk/leap (show the power), tail-flick, half-lidded judgment look, the one almost-warm look in the finale.
- **Role:** Fourth mentor (Stage 6). Teaches "in charge = composure, prioritization, delegation, doing less deliberately." Crucially, **Hades is the character who names Merlin's real gift out loud,** setting up the finale.
- **Do:** Portray him as genuinely competent, athletic, intelligent, and in command — *respected.* The humor is the contrast with Merlin, never Hades being foolish. He is always right.
- **Don't:** Make him cruel or a villain. He's imperious, not mean; his rare warmth is the point. No mocking the cat.

## THE BOYS — the heart (Anthony & Cyndie's kids)

- **Who:** The family's young boys. The reason Merlin's real job matters. They adore Merlin.
- **Look:** Warm, simple, joyful kid designs; keep them generic-but-lovable and respectful (no caricature). They appear mostly in Stage 1 (montage) and Stage 7 (finale), with a cameo squabble event in Stage 6.
- **Voice/Personality:** Pure delight. Laughter, shrieks of joy, "Merlin!!", the group hug. Their happiness is the win condition of the whole game.
- **Animation set:** running in, laughing, hugging, piling onto Merlin, the group tableau.
- **Role:** The emotional payoff. In Stage 7 their joy *is* Merlin's job.
- **Do:** Keep them warm, kind, and central to the ending. Their laughter is the loudest, happiest sound in the game.
- **Don't:** Give them conflict beyond a gentle, friendly squabble (Stage 6 event). Never in peril. Keep them age-appropriate and affectionate.

---

## ANTHONY & CYNDIE — the family (cameo)

- **Who:** The parents; the home everyone belongs to. Cyndie is the person this game is *for.* They appear at the edges — the warmth the whole world rests on.
- **Look/Role:** Brief, fond cameos (Stage 1 setup, Stage 7 tableau). Carry over any likeness/style cues from the prior game for continuity (e.g. the cheetah jacket gag if it fits). Keep affectionate and respectful.
- **Do:** Use them to frame "home" as the place Merlin's real job lives.
- **Don't:** Center the gameplay on them; they're the warm backdrop, the boys + Merlin are the focus.

---

## SUPPORTING / CARTOON OPPONENTS (Stage 3 only)

- **Garbage Goblin, Mischief Gremlin, The Mailman's Nemesis** — silly, harmless cartoon troublemakers who cause neighborhood mischief (knocked cans, stolen shoes). Purely comedic.
- **Do:** Defeat = bonk → cartoon stars → drop the loot → **run away embarrassed/laughing.** Big telegraphs, goofy designs.
- **Don't:** No injury, no blood, no K.O./death, no human cruelty. They're nuisances, not menaces. They're basically having fun too.
